In terms of getting the software to other Mac users to try out, what is the easiest way. Can I zip up applicstion in a single file and just ship it?
=======================================================================

Yes. For a small group of Mac testers, the easiest path is:
1. In Xcode, choose Product → Archive.
2. In the Organizer, choose Distribute App.
3. For informal testing, choose Direct Distribution or Copy App, depending on what Xcode offers.
4. Put the resulting .app into a folder.
5. Compress it: Finder → right-click → Compress.
6. Send the .zip.

But there is one catch: macOS Gatekeeper.
If the app is not signed/notarized, your testers may see “Apple could not verify…” or “app is damaged/can’t be opened.” They can usually bypass it by:

* right-clicking the app
* choosing Open
* confirming Open

For a few trusted testers, that may be good enough.  The smoother route is:
* join the Apple Developer Program
* sign the app with a Developer ID Application certificate
* notarize it with Apple
* zip the notarized .app
* send that zip

Then testers can open it normally.  So practically: For first experiments: yes, zip the .app and send it.  For non-technical testers: sign and notarize, or use TestFlight.

For your first DeadEnds Script Runner testers, I’d start with a zipped .app, plus a one-page note explaining the right-click → Open step.
